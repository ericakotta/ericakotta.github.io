Education


PhD in Physics

New York University (Aug 2016 - May 2022)


GPA: 3.82
Relevant Courses: Statistical mechanics, computational physics, quantum computing with noisy qubits, quantum field theory, classical dynamics, classical field theory


Studied topological insulator (TI) phase states using experimental and computational methods, with a focus on angle-resolved photoemission spectroscopy (ARPES) and electronic band structure calculations (ab initio and semi-empirical modeling). Developed a novel ARPES experimentation paradigm to achieve multi-sample doping analysis with a single sample, demonstrated with statistical analysis in Ref. [1] to achieve first ever experimental observation of hybridization in a TI. Developed open-source software implementing novel Fourier-based image filtering for ARPES data in Ref. [2].
Awarded Dean's Dissertation Fellowship 2021-2022.


Bachelors in Physics (Minor in Music)

University of California, Berkeley (Aug 2009 - May 2013)


GPA: 3.70
Relevant Courses: Quantum mechanics, advanced electronics lab


Built an optical theremin for Advanced Electronics Lab final, which was pretty rad.
As a Lab Assistant at the Space Sciences Lab, I designed and built a circuit board to detect and process magnetic and electric fields in aurora, deployed on the sounding rocket project GREECE, launched out of Fairbanks, AK in 2014 (see Ref. [3]).
